Brand Evolution Stories

How major brands adapted (or failed to adapt) to market changes

Nokia's Rise & Fall

Nokia dominated the mobile market in the early 2000s but failed to transition to smartphones, eventually selling its mobile business to Microsoft in 2014.

  • Peak: 40% global market share (2008)
  • Mistake: Stuck with Symbian while iOS/Android grew
  • Legacy: Known for durable, reliable phones

Apple's Ecosystem Strategy

Apple created a walled garden ecosystem that locks users into its products, generating massive recurring revenue from services and accessories.

  • Strategy: Premium pricing with ecosystem lock-in
  • Revenue: 50%+ from services and accessories
  • Innovation: Vertical integration of hardware/software

Samsung's Diversification

Samsung leveraged its vertical integration capabilities to compete across all price segments while pushing display and foldable innovation.

  • Advantage: In-house display and chip production
  • Strategy: Compete across all price points
  • Innovation: Foldable display leadership
