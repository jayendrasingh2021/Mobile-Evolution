Network Evolution

From 1G analog to 5G connectivity - the journey of mobile networks

1G - First Generation

1980s - Analog voice-only systems with poor voice quality and no security.

Speed: 2.4 kbps
Key Feature: Mobile voice calls
Technology: Analog signals

2G - Second Generation

1990s - Digital networks enabling SMS and basic data services.

Speed: 64 kbps
Key Feature: SMS messaging
Technology: Digital signals

3G - Third Generation

2000s - Mobile broadband enabling internet access, video calls, and mobile TV.

Speed: 2 Mbps
Key Feature: Mobile internet
Technology: Packet switching

4G - Fourth Generation

2010s - True mobile broadband with high-speed data for streaming and gaming.

Speed: 100 Mbps - 1 Gbps
Key Feature: HD video streaming
Technology: LTE, WiMAX

5G - Fifth Generation

2020s - Ultra-reliable low latency connections for IoT, autonomous vehicles, and more.

Speed: 1-10 Gbps
Key Feature: IoT and mission-critical apps
Technology: mmWave, massive MIMO

6G - Sixth Generation

2030s (Expected) - Terahertz frequencies, AI-integrated networks, and holographic communications.

Speed: 1 Tbps (projected)
Key Feature: Integrated AI and sensing
Technology: THz waves, AI-native
