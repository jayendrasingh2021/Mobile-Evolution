Iconic Phones Through Time

Visual journey of mobile design evolution from bricks to foldables

Motorola DynaTAC 8000X (1983)

The first commercial mobile phone - $3,995, 30 minutes talk time, 10 hours charging

Nokia 8110 (1996)

The "Matrix Phone" with iconic sliding cover and curved banana design

Nokia 3310 (2000)

Legendary durability, Snake game, over 126 million units sold worldwide

BlackBerry 5810 (2002)

First BlackBerry with phone capabilities, QWERTY keyboard for business emails

Motorola Razr V3 (2004)

Revolutionary ultra-thin flip phone, aluminum body, sold 130 million units

Apple iPhone (2007)

Revolutionized smartphones with multi-touch interface and mobile web browsing

HTC Dream (2008)

First Android phone with slide-out keyboard and trackball navigation

Samsung Galaxy S (2010)

Started Samsung's flagship series, became Apple's main Android competitor

iPhone 7 Plus (2016)

Introduced dual cameras enabling portrait mode and optical zoom

Samsung Galaxy Fold (2019)

First commercially successful foldable smartphone with flexible OLED display

iPhone 12 (2020)

Apple's first 5G iPhone with flat-edge design and MagSafe technology

Modern Flagship (2023)

Current generation with multi-camera systems, 5G, and AI processors
